Microwave spinach and sweet potato dal

  • Time: 1hr prep and cooking
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Red lentils 200g
Sweet potatoes, peeled and diced 2
Ground tumeric 1 tsp
Hot vegetable stock from 2 stock cubes 1 litre
Spinach 100g
Sunflower oil 2 tbsp
Cumin seeds 2tsp
Crushed chillies 1 tsp
Salt
Freshly ground black pepper
Len cut into wedges, to serve 1

Step 1: Place the lentils, sweet potatoes, turmeric and some salt in a large microwavable bowl. Stir in the hot stock, cover and microwave on high for 15 minutes, until the lentils are tender. Stir in the spinach then leave to sit for 4–5 minutes.

Step 2:Meanwhile, place the oil, cumin seeds and chillies in a small microwavable bowl. Cover and microwave on high for 2 minutes until fragrant.

Step 3:Stir the dal and season with salt and pepper. Spoon over the spiced oil and serve with lemon wedges for squeezing.

Microwave chilli bean stuffed pepper

  • Time: 1hr prep and cooking
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
Water from a kettle just boiled 250ml
Chicken or vegetable stock cube 1/2
Long grain rice 60g
Tin beans in chilli sauce 1/2 x 400g
Large pepper, any colour 1
Chedder, finely grated 25g
A pinch of dried chilli flakes, to sprinkle
Pitta breads 1 or 2
Ground black pepper

Step 1: Pour the just-boiled water into a large microwaveable bowl – it will need be large enough to hold at least 2 litres of water. Put on a microwaveable plate.

Step 2:Stir in the stock cube until dissolved, then add the rice and beans in chilli sauce. Cover with a second microwaveable plate. Cook on HIGH for 13 minutes (1000W) or 14 minutes (800W), stirring halfway. Remove from the microwave and leave to stand.

Step 3:Cut the pepper in half from the stalk to the base and scoop out and discard any seeds. Place the pepper on a microwaveable plate or shallow dish, cut-side down. Cook, uncovered, on HIGH for 4 minutes (1000W), 5 minutes (800W), or until just softened. Turn cut side up.

Step 4:Spoon the rice mixture into the pepper halves and sprinkle with the cheese. Cook, uncovered, on HIGH for about a minute or until the cheese melts. Sprinkle with chilli flakes, if using.

Step 5:Warm the pitta bread, or any other bread, on a microwaveable plate for 20–30 seconds on HIGH. (Don’t overheat or it could toughen.) Serve with the stuffed peppers.

Microwave chicken and chorizo jambalaya

  • Time: 1hr prep and cooking
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Boiling water 200ml
Tin, chopped tomatoes 200g
Onion, roughly chpooed 1/2
Celery stick, roughly chopped 1
Green pepper, seeds removed, roughly chopped 1/2
Garlic clove, grated or crushed 1
Hot sauce (optional) 1/2 tsp
Tomato puree 2 tsp
Cajun seasoning 1 1/2 tsp
Easy-cook long grain rice 125g
Cooking chorizo, skinned and roughly chopped 50g
Boneless chicken thighs, skin removed, roughly chopped 2
Roughly chopped fresh parsley 1 tbsp

Step 1: Pour the water into a heatproof jug, add the tinned tomatoes and stir well.

Step 2: Tip the onions, celery and pepper into a large microwaveable bowl, then add the garlic, hot sauce (if using), tomato purée and Cajun seasoning and mix together well.

Step 3: Add the rice and stir through so it’s coated in all the vegetables, then add the chorizo and chicken. Pour in the tomato mixture and stir well.

Step 4: Cover with cling film and cook in the microwave on high for 6 minutes (900 watt) or 8 minutes (800 watt).

Step 5: Remove and stir well, then cover again and cook for another 7 minutes (900 watt) or 9 minutes (800 watt).

Step 6: Remove and leave to stand for 5 minutes until the rice is tender, the liquid absorbed and the jambalaya piping hot.

Step 7: If the rice is not cooked fully, stir again, cover and cook on high for 2 more minutes, then check again. Repeat, if necessary, until the rice, chicken and chorizo are cooked and the jambalaya is piping hot.

Step 8: Stir in half the parsley, then divide between serving bowls, top with the remaining parsley and serve.
